Kevin O’Leary dropped from BNN, CTV News after launch of Conservative leadership ‘exploratory committee’

Facebook | Twitter | Google+ | Email

Sean Craig
Friday, Dec. 23, 2016

Businessman Kevin O'Leary hasn't decided whether he'll enter the Conservative leadership race, but that isn't stopping those already in the race from going after him. THE CANADIAN PRESS/Adrian Wyld

Bell Media announced Friday that potential Conservative leadership candidate Kevin O’Leary will no longer appear on the company’s financial television network BNN or CTV News.

The media conglomerate hired the businessman and reality TV star away from the CBC two years ago, and he has since served as a commentator on economic and political matters across its platforms. In recent months, O’Leary has been flirting with the prospect of running for the Conservative party’s top job, creating a potential conflict of interest for Bell Media news properties covering the leadership race.

Bell Media’s announcement came after O’Leary unveiled an “exploratory committee” and website Friday morning. The team backing O’Leary includes, among others, former Stephen Harper cabinet minister Marjory LeBreton and former premier of Ontario Mike Harris.

Chris Roussakis / National PostKevin O'Leary arrives for the start of the Manning Centre Conference in Ottawa on February 26, 2016

The website also contains a sign up section where Conservative Party members or members of the public can submit their personal details to the committee, a sign that the team behind O’Leary is already looking to build a supporter database for a campaign.

“As a result of today’s announcements Mr. O’Leary will no longer be a contributor to BNN or CTV News,” a Bell Media spokesperson said in a statement Friday to the National Post. That marks a change from Thursday, when Bell said it would only sever ties with O’Leary if he formally declared his entry into the race.

“He could continue to appear as a guest, but not as a contributor in any official capacity,” the Bell spokesperson added.

Chris Roussakis / National PostKevin O'Leary speaks during the Manning Centre Conference in Ottawa in February.

As well as creating a conflict of interest for newsrooms trying to cover the leadership race for Bell Media properties, O’Leary’s continued paid media appearances raised ethical concerns. “O’Leary could use his position to politically attack people for his own political gain,” Stephen Ward, a distinguished lecturer in ethics at the School of Journalism at the University of British Columbia, told the National Post on Thursday. “Whether or not he does, the problem is the public perception that this is possible, which is quite reasonable.”

In his regular appearances on BNN in recent weeks, O’Leary has been critical of Prime Minister Justin Trudeau’s carbon tax and economic policies and has also attacked NDP Premier Rachel Notley and Kathleen Wynne.

A spokesperson for his company O’Shares told the National Post Thursday that O’Leary had agreed not to make partisan statements on air, but as recently as two days earlier he had implied in a BNN appearance that Trudeau would lose the next federal election in 2019 if he didn’t abandon his government’s carbon tax policy.
